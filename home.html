fun facts about camels

  1. There are two types of camels: One humped or “dromedary” camels and two humped Bactrian camels.

  2. Camels have three sets of eyelids and two rows of eyelashes to keep sand out of their eyes.

  3. Camels have thick lips which let them forage for thorny plants other animals can't eat.

  4. Their humps let them store up to 80 pounds of fat which they can live off for weeks and even months!

  5. When a camel finally does find water, he can drink up to 40 gallons in one go.

  6. Camels are very strong and can carry up to 900 pounds for 25 miles a day.

  7. Camels can travel at up to 40 miles per hour – the same as a racehorse!

  8. Don’t make a camel angry – they can spit as a way to distract whatever they think is a threat

  9. Mother camels carry their calves up to 14 months before giving birth.

  10. Some calves are born completely white and turn brown as their adult coat comes in.

  11. There are over 160 words for camel in Arabic alone

Colorful Facts About Chameleons

  1. CHAMELEONS VARY WILDLY IN TERMS OF SIZE

  2. ALMOST HALF OF ALL KNOWN SPECIES LIVE IN MADAGASCAR

  3. THEIR FEET WORK LIKE SALAD TONGS

  4. Chameleons actual eyesight is great they can see small insects 5-10 meters away. They can also see in both visible and ultraviolet light.

  5. Chameleons feed by ballistically projecting their tongues often over twice the length of their body to catch prey, forming a suction cup as it hits its target.

  6. The chameleons tongue can reach its prey in just 0.07 split seconds, with the projectile acceleration reaching over 41 g's of force.

  7. Chameleons usually eat large insects such as locusts, grasshoppers, crickets, and stick insects, some bigger species also eat other lizards and young birds.

  8. Chameleons are not deaf but they do not actually have ear openings.

  9. Many species of chameleon have horn-like bumps or crests on their heads.

  10. Species of chameleon can be as small as 15 mm (0.59 in) or as large as 69 cm (27 in).

anonymous facts about teddy bears

  1. The Oxford English Dictionary dates the first use of the term teddy bear to 1906.

  2. The Teddy Bears' Picnic song was originally called The Teddy Bear Two Step.

  3. The term bear-hug was first recorded in 1846.

  4. Do you know what to call a person who is too fond of or loves collecting teddy bears? – Arctophile, which combines the Greek words arctos (bear) and philos (loving/fond of).

  5. The first toy bear is believed to be made by German toy maker Margarete Steiff in 1902 that was a jointed, stuffed one.

  6. However, it was the 26th President of the US, Theodore “Teddy” Roosevelt, who was the inspiration behind the first teddy bear.

  7. American multi-billionaire investor Paul Greenwood owned the largest collection of teddy bears.

  8. In 2010, the US government auctioned his collection of 1300 teddy bears at Christie’s for £1.1 million.

  9. Japanese company Fujitsu has manufactured a teddy bear robot for adults that can make simple gestures and even small talk with humans.

  10. The term “bear hug” meaning a tight embrace was first used in 1846, almost 60 years before teddy bear was “born”.

  11. It was English Romantic writer Robert Southey who brought bears into the literary scene with the fairy-tale ‘Goldilocks and the Three Bears’ in 1834.
